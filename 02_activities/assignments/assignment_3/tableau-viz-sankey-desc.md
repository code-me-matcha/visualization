
- For each visualization, describe and justify: 
    > What software did you use to create your data visualization?
        I used Tableau to create my Sankey diagram, specifically the Sankey web extension to streamline creation of the viz.
    > Who is your intended audience? 
        My intended audience includes Ontario government officials, funding recipients (municipalities, organizations), community stakeholders, and emergency first-responders (firefighters, police departments, etc). This audience needs transparency on funding allocation.
    > What information or message are you trying to convey with your visualization? 
        My viz conveys how Community Emergency Preparedness Grant (CEPG) funds flow from the Ontario government to recipients (ie municipalities, emergency services) and their uses (ie equipment, training).
    > What aspects of design did you consider when making your visualization? How did you apply them? With what elements of your plots? 
        - Clarity: Limited nodes/links, grouping small recipients into the "Other" category for simplicity
        - Colour: High-contrast colours for readability
        - Interactivity: User can use the hover tooltips to show funding amounts
        - Proportionality: Link widths reflect the funding amount/size
        - Layout: Organized from left-to-right flow for intuitiveness, accessibility, and engagement
    > How did you ensure that your data visualizations are reproducible? If the tool you used to make your data visualization is not reproducible, how will this impact your data visualization? 
        Tableau is not reproducible, as such this will decrease the reliability and accessibility of my viz. It may be less effective in communicating my message and getting buy-in as readers cannot validate the data I used or retrace the steps I took to create the viz. 
    
    > How did you ensure that your data visualization is accessible?  
        I used high-contrast colours (blue, red) for colourblind users, legible fonts, and included the interactive hover tooltips. Alt text is included to support screen readers. 
    
    > Who are the individuals and communities who might be impacted by your visualization? 
        - Government officials: gain insights into the allocation of the CEPG funding
        - Funding recipients: gain better understanding of the share of funding that they receive
        - Community stakeholders/advocates: gain insights into the share of funding they receive, better enabling and positioning them to advocate for their needs
        - First responders: better understand what resources the community has for support, and can better determine how they should allocate their own resources in cases of emergency depending on what resources those communities have in place already 
    
    > How did you choose which features of your chosen dataset to include or exclude from your visualization?
        I chose to include the following features: funding sources, recipient categories, amounts, and use of the funding (expenditure category). For the recipient categories and expenditure categories, I grouped them by common occurrences for simplicity and readability. 

        I chose to exclude minor recipients and non-funding metrics to avoid clutter, focusing on clear flow and visualization. 
    
    > What ‘underwater labour’ contributed to your final data visualization product?
        Underwater labour that contributed to my final data viz product includes data collection from government soruces, cleaning (standardizing formats, grouping free-form responses or sentence responses in the 'Description' column), exploratory analysis, and testing iterations. These ensure accuracy and relevance but are often unseen. Additionally, underwater labour was also required for the Sankey Tableau viz extension to be created, which greatly streamlined the process of making this chart in Tableau as the chart was not a readily available chart type.

      Bad Visualization: Titanic survival rates (source: https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/neil.cotie/viz/TitanicAlluvialDiagram/TitanicDoubleAlluvial)

      This visualization is classified as bad as it does not align with most or any of the 3 important qualities of data viz:
      1)  Is the visualization pleasing to look at? → Aesthetic: No, it is not. The colours are the default Tableau colours, and the maker also used the same colours consistently across each column/category of data, which is confusing and unpleasing. There are few to no data labels, nor a description to make it more explanatory for the reader to follow along. 
      2) Does the visualization accurately and honestly present data? → Substantive: Yes, it appears to present it accurately and honestly as the maker is not attempting to sway the reader into an opinion.
      3) Can we understand what message the maker of the visualization is attempting to convey? → Perceptual: Yes/somewhat - the data is simplistic, just like the visualization, so I am able to understand that the maker is trying to convey the Titanic survival rates based on different categories and sub-categories. 

      It also fails to meet most of the Gestalt principles:
      1) Proximity: N/A
      2) Similarity: No - the maker used the same colours across different categories, violating this principle
      3) Continuity: No - the maker used the same colours across different categories which suggests continuity where there isn't - violating this principle
      4) CLosure: N/A
      5) Enclosure: N/A
      6) Connection: See comments above on same colours across different categories

      Good Visualization: European asylum seeker application decisions (source: http://www.economist.com/blogs/graphicdetail/2015/05/daily-chart-1?fsrc=scn/tw/te/bl/ed/seeking_safety&%3Ffsrc%3Dscn/=tw/dc)
      
      This visualization is classified as good as it aligns with all of the 3 important qualities of data viz:
      1)  Is the visualization pleasing to look at? → Aesthetic: Yes, it is. This viz is beautifully made, with colours that are cohesive, not clashing, and attracting my attention to the right places. The data is well-labelled and the figure has a title and description, making it easier on the eyes and reducing cognitive dissonance. 
      2) Does the visualization accurately and honestly present data? → Substantive: Yes, it appears to present it accurately and honestly as the maker is not attempting to sway the reader into an opinion.
      3) Can we understand what message the maker of the visualization is attempting to convey? → Perceptual: Yes - there is plenty of data labelling at each branch/data point, making it clear and easy to understand the message.

      1) Proximity: N/A

      3) Continuity: Yes - the maker makes it clear which branches are continuing where
      4) CLosure: N/A
